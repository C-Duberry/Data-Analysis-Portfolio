### Project 2: Global CO₂ Emission Trends

Created an interactive Tableau dashboard to explore global CO₂ emissions trends, highlighting changes over time, differences between countries, the relationship between population and emissions, and fossil fuel contributions.

   <img src="https://github.com/C-Duberry/Data-Analysis-Portfolio/blob/main/c02-world.PNG?raw=true" alt="[Global C02 Emmission Trends]" >
   
[View dashboard](https://public.tableau.com/views/GlobalCOGHGEmissionsDashboard17502024/Dashboard1?:language=en-GB&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)  

#### Findings

- The United States has the highest cumulative CO₂ emissions in the dataset from 1924–2024, highlighting the impact of long-term industrialisation and energy consumption. Although China is one of the largest current annual emitters, the United States has the highest total emissions across the full historical period analysed.
- Global CO₂ emissions have increased steadily over the last century, with more significant growth occurring in recent decades.
- Countries with larger populations generally produce higher total CO₂ emissions, although population size is not the only factor influencing emissions levels.
- Coal is the largest contributor among the fossil fuel sources analysed, followed by oil and gas.

### Project 2: Cost of Living Analysis UK

Created an interactive Power BI dashboard to analyse UK cost of living trends, comparing salaries, rent pressure, and affordability differences across locations.

   <img src="https://github.com/C-Duberry/Data-Analysis-Portfolio/blob/main/uk-cost-living.PNG?raw=true" alt="[Cost of Living Analysis UK]" >

 [View dashboard](https://app.powerbi.com/view?r=eyJrIjoiMWE1MjZmYTYtNzQ1Yy00OGRkLWE0ZjAtN2M3YjM2MTNkNjM4IiwidCI6ImU2MzRiNjZmLWYyOTUtNDdmOS05YzQ0LTc3Y2QwYzBjM2YyYSJ9)

#### Findings   

- London has the highest average salary in the dataset at approximately £40.7k, while most UK locations fall within the £25k–£31k salary range.
- Average salaries have increased across UK locations between 2010 and 2024, showing overall wage growth over time.
- Lower salary locations generally showed lower rent as a percentage of income, suggesting that higher salaries do not always translate into greater affordability due to increased housing costs. Barnet and Bromley were notable exceptions, combining relatively high salaries with lower rent pressure.
- Barnet and Bromley were among the most affordable locations analysed, while London showed the highest cost pressures due to higher living expenses.
- The salary versus cost of living comparison shows a positive relationship, with higher salaries generally associated with higher living costs. This suggests that increased earnings in some areas may be offset by greater expenses.
